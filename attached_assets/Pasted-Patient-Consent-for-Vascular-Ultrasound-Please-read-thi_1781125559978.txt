Patient Consent for Vascular Ultrasound
Please read this information carefully before your ultrasound
A vascular ultrasound is a non-invasive test that uses sound waves to assess blood flow and blood vessels. It does not use ionising radiation.
For some vascular ultrasound examinations, you will be asked to partially disrobe so the area being examined can be accessed properly. This may include removal of shoes, socks, compression stockings, and trousers or other clothing as needed. A gown will be provided for your comfort and privacy.
During the examination, a sonographer will place ultrasound gel on your skin and move an ultrasound probe over the area being examined. For patient safety, ultrasound gel is handled in accordance with infection control procedures and is stored appropriately at room temperature rather than being routinely kept in a warmer.
Depending on the type of ultrasound, gentle or firm pressure may sometimes be needed to obtain clear images. During the ultrasound, you may hear pulsing, whooshing, or other sounds from the ultrasound machine. This is normal and helps assess blood flow. Some examinations may involve scanning sensitive areas such as the groin, neck or abdomen depending on the referral and clinical indication.
Vascular ultrasound is generally a safe procedure. You may experience mild discomfort from probe pressure, particularly if the area is tender. The sonographer will take care to minimise gel contact with your clothing. Hand towels will be used to remove the gel during and after the examination. Despite this, a small amount of gel may occasionally come into contact with your clothing. The ultrasound gel is water-based, does not stain, and can be removed with a normal wash.
While every effort is made to obtain complete and accurate information, the examination has limitations and may not answer every clinical question.
Your images and clinical information will be collected for the purposes of your care, reporting, administration, billing, accreditation, quality assurance, and other uses permitted or required by law. Your information will be handled in accordance with applicable privacy obligations.
If you have any questions, would like more information, or would prefer to speak with a staff member before proceeding, please let reception or the sonographer know.
